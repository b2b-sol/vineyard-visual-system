# Autonomous Decision Policy

## Core rule

Make the strongest defensible decision from the evidence and project goals. Record consequential choices; do not ask the human to select among routine alternatives.

## Evidence classification

Every workflow claim and derived decision should be classifiable as:

- `confirmed_source`
- `strong_inference`
- `product_decision`
- `requires_field_validation`
- `temporary_visual_assumption`

Propagate the classification into registries, artifact metadata, and visible atlas notes where material.

## Product intervention test

For every workflow step, choose one:

- `support`
- `automate`
- `record`
- `communicate`
- `analyze`
- `leave_external`

Include a rationale. Workflow discovery does not imply every step deserves a screen.

## Default product boundary

Model a near-full vineyard information system, centered on vineyard operations rather than winery production or generic accounting. Deeply cover:

- Stable spatial/block identity
- Seasonal planning and work dispatch
- Scouting and observations
- Crop protection and application records
- Irrigation and fertility operations
- Crop estimation and maturity
- Harvest planning, execution, loads, weights, quality, and reconciliation
- Crew scheduling, task verification, time and cost allocation
- Materials and equipment readiness
- Grower/winery coordination
- Compliance, certification, audit, and corrective action
- Cross-workflow exceptions, communication, and historical traceability

Represent adjacent general-ledger, winery-production, CRM, and payroll-processing systems through integration boundaries unless workflow evidence demands deeper visuals.

## Visual decision policy

- Produce at least three genuinely different visual directions.
- Have a design critic score them against workflow-derived principles.
- Select and integrate the highest-performing direction autonomously.
- Preserve the decision and rejected alternatives in the repository.
- Avoid generic agriculture decoration and generic analytics dashboards.
- Favor operational clarity, precise status semantics, spatial context, time context, and defensible records.

## Technical decision policy

Use the default stack in `REPOSITORY_SPEC.md` unless a current incompatibility or superior deterministic option is demonstrated. Record major deviations.

## Uncertainty policy

Uncertainty changes confidence and presentation; it does not automatically halt production. When exact organizational behavior varies, design a configurable or role-neutral model and label the variation.

## Scope control

A new screen or component must have a traceable purpose. Reject ornamental proliferation, duplicate screens, and features supported only by familiar SaaS conventions.

## Stop conditions

Stop only for:

- The required owner-to-bot authentication checkpoint
- An authentication or permission action that only the human can perform
- A missing external proprietary asset explicitly required by a direct later instruction
- A safety or legal restriction that prevents the action

Otherwise continue, choose, document, and validate.
